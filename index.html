Optics

Problematics and solutions

A first try

As in every opto-mechanical system, optics have been the leading factor in the mechanical design of our glasses.
We first investigated the different possible solutions, which are listed in our State of the Art.
We focused on the solution that seems to be used in Google Glass, which is the only affordable and DIY-able one. Our idea was to adapt it to a cheap, low-res display (as we could obviously not afford a Google Glass Display which is 1000 times denser than a Retina Display).
This amazing work helped us to do just that. In conclusion, here is the commonly proposed optical scheme that we followed : The next step towards prototype realisation was defining the screen we were going to use. Our intended goal being only the design of a proof of concept, we chose a simple 128*32px display available on AdaFruit.
This led us to a first design which proved to a complete failure, due to our bad knowledge of 3D printing rules :

The idea was, as in Google Glass, to put the display at the focal plane of a spheric mirror to form its image at infinity in order for the eye not to need to accomodate. In this model, the display is put on the top of the eye, the image is projected vertically and reflected on the 45° beamsplitter after its reflection on the spherical mirror at the bottom of the apparatus.

A more viable prototype

This first prototype was simply not rigid enough to be tested in any way, which led us to design a new, more solid one, based on the same optical system :

This time, the image is displayed horizontally, in a way much similar to what happens in Google Glass prism : The display placed at the right side of the right eye enlightens the spherical mirror placed in front, which reflects the image on the beam splitter which directs 50% of coming light onto the user's retina.

However, finding cheap and adapted spherical mirror was not easy. Therefore we decided to buy reflective adhesive film and try to stick it on the adapted spherical surface. This was a fail due to the rigidity of the film and we could not manage to obtain a satisfying spherical mirror. However, we are pretty convinced that with an adapted spherical mirror, this prototype would have been fully functional.

The final prototype

We were forced to admit that with the time and means which were allocated to this project, we could simply not achieve a functional design based on a spherical mirror. Hence, we decided to propose another, final design where the optical system is based on a focusing lens which is both cheaper and easier to procure. However, this will obviously be done at the expense of aesthetics because of the dimensions of the lens we chose ( Ø 5mm, focal length 10mm ).

Here, one can see that the display is placed at the bottom of a 10cm long tube at the end of which is located the 10 dioptres focusing lens. Light path is 10cm long from the lens to the beam splitter, so that the image fits perfectly on the beam splitter.
